Personal info

Here is my current CV .

I grew up in Shanghai, China, a city near the Yangtze river. My name in Chinese writing is 王含.

I went to the University of Science and Technology of China in Hefei for my undergraduate studies, where I developed an interest in physics. Then I went to New York, USA, where I earned a Ph.D. from the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences under the supervision of Oliver Bühler. There, I was exposed to beauties in geophysics fluid dynamics; a fateful event was learning about the power laws in the Gage-Nastrom spectrum and its (dis)connections with classical two-dimentional theories， My PhD work developed some analytical methods that can help us gain dynamical insights from in-situ observational data. My journey continued at University of Toronto, where I spent 1.5 years working with Nicolas Grisouard, mainly exploring applications of deep learning to disentangling balanced and unbalances flows. Since April 2022, I started my second postdoc at University of Edinburgh, working with Jacques Vanneste and remotely with William R. Young on interactions between deep-water surface gravity waves and balanced flows.
